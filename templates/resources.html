Resources for UXers

I can't vouch for all of these resources, but I've used some of them, and have heard good things about the other ones. This list was last updated in January 2025.

Platforms for portfolios

Sometimes I see UX portfolios built in slide decks (Microsoft PowerPoint, Google Slides, Figma, Canva, etc.) and occasionally see portfolios coded from scratch (e.g., HTML/CSS, JavaScript) and hosted on free platforms (e.g., GitHub pages, Netlify, PythonAnywhere).

But most often I see portfolios built using these tools:

  • Webflow.io
  • Framer.website
  • Adobe Portfolio
  • Wix Studio
  • uxfol.io
  • Notion.site
Education & work experience

You can learn about UX from a free bootcamp/online classes, paid ones, or while earning a graduate degree, but no matter which you persue, it will likely still be challenging to get your first job in UX. Because of this, I recommend you don't spend much money on your UX education. Strong graduate programs are amazing, but I'm not sure if they're worth taking out huge loans if you may struggle to get a job afterwards.

One reason why it is so difficult to get your first UX job is because recruiters and hiring managers know that one of the hardest parts of UX is stakeholder management, and very few school or bootcamp projects have real stakeholders, let alone opportunities for you to create real business impact. One way to get that experience is by volunteering to do UX for a real organization!

  • Catch-a-Fire Work pro-bono (unpaid) for nonprofits to gain work experience that you can (usually?) showcase in your portfolio.
  • Tech Fleet Join a group of other new-to-their-industry professionals and gain work experience that you can (usually?) showcase in your portfolio.
  • UX Rescue This community was very active in 2020, but I haven't heard much about them since then.
